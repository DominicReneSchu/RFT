# Global Wealth Gap – Estimating Development through the Resonance Reactor

## Introduction and Initial Situation

The global wealth gap is currently wide open:  
- The richest 10 % of the world’s population own about 76 % of global wealth (cf. World Inequality Report 2022).  
- Over 700 million people live in extreme poverty (< 2 USD per day; cf. World Bank, 2024).  
- Access to energy is a key factor for economic development, education, medical care, and quality of life (cf. IEA, 2022).

## The Impact of the Resonance Reactor

- **Energy prices (electricity, heat):** Local price drop (up to 90 %) at pilot sites.
- **Production costs:** Significant reduction across all industries, with start-ups and SMEs especially benefiting.

### Long-Term Effects (15–30 Years)

- **Closing the Wealth Gap:**

  - Migration out of necessity drops dramatically—people voluntarily remain in their home regions.

  - The economic basis for global conflicts (resources, energy, poverty) shrinks.

- **Global Stability:**
