## 1. Energy as a Directed Vector

Classically, energy is directionless. In the RFT, energy is a
directed vector in energy coupling space:

- **Magnitude** (|E|): Classical energy quantity
- **Direction** (ê_Res): Orientation in coupling space, determines
  transfer direction and efficiency
- **Intrinsic sense of rotation (spin):** Coupling and transfer are
  influenced by the sense of rotation (Axiom 5)

The visible example is **torque**:

```
    T = r × F    [Nm] = [J]
```

- Magnitude: classical energy
- Direction: determined by the right-hand rule
- Sense of rotation: expression of the coupling structure

Torque is the spatial projection of a directed energy vector (Axiom 5).

## 2. Spin as an Expression of Resonance Rotation

**Quantum mechanics:** Spin is a quantized, intrinsic
angular momentum without a classical analogue (electron: spin-½,
720° symmetry).

**Resonance Field Theory:** Spin is the closed rotation of the
energy vector in a higher dimension (Axiom 5).

- Spin quantum numbers characterize coupling states
- The spin operator: S_z |ψ⟩ = s·ℏ |ψ⟩

## 3. Direction-Dependent Resonance Transfer

Two systems can only efficiently exchange energy when their
energy vectors are resonantly coupled:

```
    K = K₀ · cos(θ)
```

- K: Coupling strength
- K₀: Maximum coupling (at θ = 0°)
- θ: Angle between energy vectors

Only at θ = 0° is maximum transfer possible.

**Applications:**
- **Mechanics:** Coupled pendulums oscillate synchronously when
  their energy vectors are aligned
- **Quantum communication:** A photon transfers information only with
  matching polarization to the detector axis
- **Biophysics:** FRET works only with matching orientation
  of donor and acceptor
- **Electrical engineering:** Polarization filters as directional filters
  for energy vectors

## 4. Momentum and Energy as a Vectorial Pair

### Classical Physics

```
    p = m · v        (vector)
    E = ½ m v²       (scalar)
```

### Resonance Field Theory

```
    E⃗ = |E| · ê_Res        (vector)
    E⃗ ∥ p  or  E⃗ antiparallel to p
```

The direction ê_Res is determined by the coupling structure of the
resonance field (Axioms 4, 5, 6). For photons this shows in
the relationship between momentum direction, polarization, and spin.

Energy is a vector with a higher-dimensional sense of rotation. Its
