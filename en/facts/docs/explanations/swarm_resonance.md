## 1. The Known

Before applying RFT, let us gather what is already understood:

**Lift through wing shape:** Difference in flow speeds above and below a wing
creates a pressure difference that generates lift (Bernoulli effect). The wing
shape determines how much faster the air flows on one side.

**Flow mechanics:** Reynolds similarity describes how fluid flows behave at
different scales and speeds. A small bird and a large aircraft can operate in
aerodynamically similar regimes despite their vastly different sizes.

**Swarm behavior:** Empirically well observed, but the mechanics behind it —
why swarms are so extremely coordinated and agile — is not fully understood.
Existing models (Boids, Reynolds rules: Separation, Alignment, Cohesion)
describe the **pattern** but not the physical mechanism. They tell us *what*
the birds do, but not *why* it works so well physically.
