## 3. The Coupling Operator (𝜀): Nature, Function, and Interpretation

### 3.1 Definition and Properties

The **coupling operator** 𝜀 is a central quantity of Resonance Field Theory. It describes the ability of two systems to connect via resonance—energetically, informationally, or structurally.

Unlike classical physical constants, 𝜀 is **not a fixed number** but a **dynamic measure of coupling**. It depends on:

- Frequency ratios (f₁, f₂)
- Phase and coherence
- Oscillation quality (e.g., damping, order)
- Geometric or structural resonance
- Space-time proximity within the resonance field

**Example:**  
Coupled pendulums: 𝜀 increases when frequencies and phases are close.  
Quantum oscillators: 𝜀 is maximized in coherent superposition.  
Neural networks: 𝜀 increases with synchrony and network connectivity.

### 3.2 Function in the Resonance Field Equation

### 3.3 Natural Resonance Interval

Although 𝜀 is not a fixed value, a **natural resonance interval** can be stated:

𝜀 replaces the classical coupling constant with a resonance-dependent, dynamic measure—a measure of the willingness of two systems to become one through resonance.
