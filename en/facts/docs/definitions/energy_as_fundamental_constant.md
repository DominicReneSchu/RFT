## 1. Introduction

Resonance Field Theory (Axiom 4) describes the effective energy
of a resonant coupling as:

```
    E_eff = π · ε(Δφ) · h · f
```

This document explores the hypothesis that energy is the
fundamental quantity from which all other physical quantities
can be derived. This hypothesis builds on Axioms A1–A7, but
is an interpretative extension — not an additional axiom.

## 2. Derivation of Physical Quantities from Energy

Starting from the resonance field equation E = π·ε·h·f, known
physical relations can be represented as resonance states:

| Quantity | Derivation from E | Resonance interpretation |
|----------|-------------------|--------------------------|
| Time t | t = 1/f | Inverse of the cyclic coupling frequency |
| Momentum p | p = E/c | Directed energy flow (Axiom 5) |
| Mass m | m = E/c² | Resonance projection of stationary coupling states |
| Temperature T | T = E/k_B | Thermodynamic measure of energy distribution |
| Entropy S | S = -x·ln(x) | Order measure of a resonance configuration |

### 2.1 Time as Coupling Period

From Axiom 1 (every entity oscillates periodically) it follows:

```
    f = 1/t    →    t = 1/f
```

Time is the inverse coupling frequency. In the complex
time structure (see [τ* coefficient](../mathematics/tau_resonance_coefficient.md)):

```
    t = t_r + i·t_i = cos(α)·t + i·sin(α)·t
```

### 2.2 Mass as Coupling State

The equivalence E = mc² is interpreted in the RFT as:

```
    m = E/(c²) = π·ε·h·f/(c²)
```

Mass is a resonance state with maximum localization
(minimal frequency width, standing wave).

### 2.3 Temperature as Coupling Distribution

```
    T = E/k_B = π·ε·h·f/k_B
```

Temperature describes the mean energy per degree of freedom —
in the RFT the mean coupling energy of a configuration.

## 3. Wave Function and Coupling Structure

The general wave function of a resonance state:

```
    Ψ(x, t) = A · e^{i(kx − ωt)}
```

with E = π·ε·h·f. All couplings (explicit and implicit) are
incorporated through superposition (Axiom 2) and the resonance condition
(Axiom 3).

**Examples:**
- **Photon:** E = h·f (special case ε = 1/π), p = h/λ
- **Molecular vibration:** E_n = h·f·(n + 1/2), T = E_n/k_B
- **Thermal state:** E = k_B·T, Ψ as phonon field

## 5. Delimitation

This document describes an **interpretative hypothesis**,
not an axiomatic extension. The derivations (m = E/c²,
T = E/k_B, etc.) are well-known physical relations
that are formulated here in the language of the RFT. They
do not formally follow from A1–A7, but use the RFT notation
for reinterpretation.
