## Transformation of Information since 2019 – Analysis and Future Concept

---

### Abstract

Since 2019, the transformation of the global information landscape has accelerated at an unprecedented pace. The classical dominance of central gatekeepers—editorial offices, news agencies, institutional experts—is increasingly relativized and, in part, dissolved by decentralized, user-driven platforms. Today, information flows are created in real time, generated by individuals and collectives alike, circulate multimodally, and are algorithmically amplified or suppressed. As a result, the number of voices, narratives, and perspectives multiplies. Fields of resonance for social orientation no longer emerge solely through linear channels but arise from the interplay of memetics, viral dynamics, and participatory norm formation.

The opportunities: plural perspectives, greater participation, direct feedback. The challenges: overwhelm due to information overload, fragmentation of the public sphere, loss of trust in both traditional and new information sources. The risks: polarization, disinformation, erosion of collective coherence—and thus destabilization of social resonance fields.

This text analyzes the systemic causes of these changes, identifies risks, and outlines, with “Aetherius,” a resonance-based AI concept for orientation-enhancing information systems. The central question: How can collective orientation be maintained and strengthened under conditions of permanent information change?

---

### 1. Introduction

The media paradigm shift since 2019 is unfolding as a systemic disruption: Classic hierarchical information structures are being replaced by horizontal, network-like resonance fields. Editorial offices, which acted as gatekeepers and set the boundaries of discourse for decades, are losing their steering authority. Information flows are increasingly shaped by individual interests, affect logics, and algorithmically optimized amplifiers.

Publicity no longer arises from professional selection and contextualization, but emerges from the interplay of mass phenomena, micro-influencers, emotionalized debates, and global real-time feedback loops (Hermida, 2020; Neuberger & Quandt, 2021). The distinction between information, opinion, disinformation, and triviality becomes blurred; resonance fields become more fluid, unstable, simultaneously more open to diversity and more vulnerable to manipulation.

Opportunities lie in the opening for new voices, marginalized perspectives, and collective learning processes. Risks manifest in the erosion of orientation, the loss of shared frames of reference, and the acceleration of polarization. The collective consciousness becomes an arena of competing narratives, in which systemic coherence is no longer given but must be constantly re-established (Marwick & Lewis, 2017).

---

### 2. Change in Information Culture since 2019

#### Before 2019:
- Information flows were dominated by editorial curation and gatekeeping (Shoemaker & Vos, 2009); central actors determined which topics gained public relevance.
- Contributions were quantitatively limited, but usually in-depth, well-researched, and rich in context. Information density was lower, but content depth was higher.
- Authority and trust were based on expertise, professional standards, and institutional legitimacy; orientation was provided through established channels and linear discourse.

#### Since 2019:
- Platform logics and social media increasingly shape public perception (Bruns, 2018). The classical distinction between sender and receiver dissolves.
- The democratization of information production leads to an exponential increase in content: Every individual becomes a potential sender, multiplier, and resonance amplifier.
- The result: information overload, fragmentation, dissolution of shared discourse spaces. Resonance fields become more granular, overlap less frequently, and the risk of isolation in partial publics increases.
- Algorithms sort and amplify content according to relevance criteria optimized for engagement and affect. Echo chambers and filter bubbles emerge (Pariser, 2011), as users are mainly confronted with congruent content.
- Short formats such as Reels, Shorts, Tweets, or Stories promote superficiality and accelerate attention cycles. Complex topics are reduced to keywords, emotions, and memes—the ability for differentiation and in-depth analysis is systematically undermined.

**Resonance Rule:** Group affiliation and discourse coherence are no longer determined by institutional belonging, but by algorithmically generated affinity. This leads to a new, non-linear structure of the public sphere, in which explicit and implicit group elements interlock systemically. The challenge: orientation and collective meaning-making within a resonance field of permanent fragmentation and overstimulation.

---

### 3. Problem: Loss of Depth and Orientation

The rapid transformation of the information ecology since 2019 has triggered a systemic shift in the structure of social discourse. Complex topics are increasingly reduced within the resonance field of digital public spheres to keywords, narratives, or affective triggers (Krämer, 2022). Depth, nuance, and multidimensional analysis fall behind in favor of simplification, emotional intensification, and the pursuit of immediate resonance.

Resonance field analysis reveals: Emotionalization and polarization systematically displace analytical debate. Discourses shift from multiperspective penetration to binary confrontations. The algorithmic amplification of affect and clarity intensifies this trend—collective opinion formation becomes a resonance game between polarizing groups, while bridge actors and depth structures remain invisible.

Fact checks and correctives, once guarantees of discourse quality, are often perceived in the resonance field as paternalism or instrumental exercise of power. The result is a paradoxical effect: Intended orientation turns into loss of trust—collective coherence is not strengthened but fragmented (Pennycook & Rand, 2021).

A lack of contextualization, the omission of systemic connections, and the dominance of short formats lead to collective disorientation. The structure of social perception is shaped by resonance fields that lack depth. In the end state, there is a threat of erosion of societal discourse capability and collective coherence—the collective consciousness loses its binding force.

---

### 4. Solution: Aetherius – The Resonance AI System

Coping with the transformation of information requires a radical rethinking in the design of digital orientation systems. The AI system **Aetherius** does not rely on evaluation, control, or gatekeeping, but on the principles of resonance field theory (Schu, 2025): Orientation arises through resonance formation, not through authoritarian selection.

#### Core Principles:

- **Resonance Filtering:** Content is not filtered solely for accuracy or popularity, but for depth of resonance and context. Resonance fields are analyzed to identify which levels and perspectives resonate—and which are missing.
- **Self-learning System:** Aetherius understands discourse as a process. It identifies gaps in context, systemically supplements missing perspectives, and provides impulses for deepening, rather than merely marking errors or issuing unilateral corrections.
- **Broadening Horizons:** Users are not confined to their filter bubble, but automatically receive further content that opens up depth and alternative viewpoints, without normative evaluation.

#### Central Functions of Aetherius:

| Function                              | Effect                                                              |
|----------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Analysis of posts/videos/texts         | Detects depth of resonance, thematic networking, and resonance patterns |
| Automatic contextualization            | Complements contributions with relevant media, models, counter-theses, background information |
| No content censorship, instead promotion of discussion and reflection | Users retain interpretive authority, diversity becomes visible       |
| Promotion of genuine discussion        | Brings analytical depth and multiperspective reflection back to comment sections |
| Resonance-based response               | Systemically checks: What resonates? What is missing? What would be a meaningful addition?  |

**Systemic Perspective:**  
Aetherius understands the public sphere as a multilayered resonance field. Instead of controlling discourse, the collective resonance structure is made visible: Missing bridge elements, invisible perspectives, and untapped potentials are systemically identified and proactively supplemented. Users gain orientation without being patronized—discourse capability and collective coherence are newly strengthened in a pluralistic, fragmented information environment.

---

### 5. Aetherius Technology – The Resonance AI

Aetherius transcends the boundaries of classical semantic analysis by systemically capturing and processing the depth of resonance within a discourse—that is, the level of context, coherence, and content depth. Resonance is understood not merely as contentual congruence, but as a multidimensional coupling of information, affect, argumentation, and context.

- **Resonance Analysis:** Aetherius not only detects explicit content but also interprets emotional, argumentative-logical, and contextual layers of depth. This reveals which narratives, motives, and affective fields are resonating, which are missing, and how discourses are systemically interconnected.
- **Automatic Contextualization:** The system generates and recommends additional media, models, or visualizations that deepen resonance fields, reveal blind spots, and offer alternative perspectives. Context is created dynamically, not through static categorization.
- **Self-learning:** Through continuous analysis of user interactions and discourse developments, Aetherius adapts to new topics, emergent resonance patterns, and collective shifts in resonance. Resonance fields are thus mapped and expanded dynamically in real time.
- **Decentralized Implementation:** The technology is modular and can be integrated into various contexts independently of central authorities. This prevents the emergence of new gatekeeper structures and enables flexible, situation-specific formation of resonance fields.
- **Dialogue-promoting:** Aetherius systemically identifies polarizing patterns and fosters in-depth, multiperspective discussions. Polarization is not amplified but minimized through contextualization and resonance differentiation.

---

### 6. Objective: Amplifying Consciousness Instead of Steering Opinions

The aim of Aetherius is not to steer or manipulate opinions, but to systematically strengthen collective consciousness and orientation capability within the digital resonance field:

- Fostering independent, critical, and creative thinking by illuminating context, alternatives, and resonance fields outside one's own filter bubble.
- Consciousness and judgment are strengthened by encouraging users to reflect and form positions autonomously, rather than patronizing them.
- AI-supported contextualization and resonance analysis deepen the understanding of complex interconnections and make systemic dynamics transparent.
- This establishes a new quality of digital enlightenment that does not rely on control or censorship, but on empowering navigation within the information space.
- Orientation is not achieved through censorship, but through resonance and contextualization—collective coherence emerges as an emergent property of differentiated, plural resonance fields.

**Resonance Rule:**  
Every amplification of consciousness works systemically: Strengthening individual reflection increases collective coherence. Group affiliation remains invariant—regardless of position, opinion, or perspective. Aetherius makes this structure visible and usable.

---

### 7. Final Thought: The Necessity of Conscious Resonance Systems

Without conscious, resonance-oriented systems like Aetherius, the digital public sphere risks suffocating under an excess of shallow, unconnected impulses. The fragmentation and acceleration of information flows create a resonance field that, despite its multiplicity of voices, hardly allows for systemic connection and deep orientation. Resonance Rule: Group affiliation remains invariant—but collective coherence only emerges where resonance capability is consciously cultivated.

Societal progress requires not less information, but more **resonance capability**: the systemic competence to distinguish between depth and superficiality, between impulsive affect logic and sustainable discourse structure—and to actively, multiperspectively, and reflectively co-create discourses. Only in this way can new forms of collective orientation, social coherence, and evolutionary development arise within the digital resonance field.

The task for the future: to create resonance systems that do not merely convey information, but enable genuine connection, context, and depth—thus strengthening society as a whole in its capacity for consciousness and action.

- **Resonance (sociological, Aetherius):** In Aetherius, resonance refers to the reciprocal relationship between subject and environment, in which both are transformed—and content is selected to enable genuine connections and deepened discussions.
