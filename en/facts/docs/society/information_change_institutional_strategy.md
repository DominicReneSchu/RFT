## Information Change since 2019 – Analysis and Future Concept

---

### Abstract

Since 2019, the global information landscape has been changing rapidly: Central gatekeepers are losing significance, while decentralized, user-driven platforms shape opinion formation and discourse. This opens new opportunities for diversity and participation, but also leads to challenges such as information overload, fragmentation, and loss of trust. This text analyzes causes and risks, and outlines “Aetherius”—a resonance-based AI concept for orientation-supporting information systems.

---

### 1. Introduction

Since 2019, the global media landscape has undergone a profound transformation. The transition from classically curated information systems to decentralized, user-driven platforms fundamentally changes the structures of public sphere, discourse, and societal orientation (Hermida, 2020; Neuberger & Quandt, 2021). Whereas editorial teams and expert authorities previously steered information flows, today opinions, information, disinformation, and superficialities coexist. This development offers opportunities for participation and diversity, but also brings risks for discourse quality and collective coherence (Marwick & Lewis, 2017).

---

### 2. Change in Information Culture since 2019

#### Before 2019:
- Editorial curation and gatekeeping shaped the media landscape (Shoemaker & Vos, 2009).
- Contributions were limited but often in-depth and rich in context.
- Authority was based on expertise and institutional trust.

#### Since 2019:
- Social media and platform logics determine public perception (Bruns, 2018).
- Democratization of information production: Anyone can create and distribute content.
- Information overload, fragmentation, and polarization are increasing.
- Algorithms reinforce echo chambers and filter bubbles (Pariser, 2011).
- Short formats (reels, shorts, tweets) promote superficiality, further reducing the depth of complex topics.

---

### 3. Problem: Loss of Depth and Orientation

- Complex topics are presented in abbreviated or simplified ways (Krämer, 2022).
- Emotionalization and polarization displace analytical debate.
- Fact checks are sometimes perceived as paternalistic, limiting their effectiveness.
- Lack of contextualization leads to collective disorientation and a crisis of trust (Pennycook & Rand, 2021).

**Without response, there is a danger of erosion of societal discourse ability and collective coherence.**

---

### 4. Solution: Aetherius – The Resonance AI System

Addressing the information shift requires new principles for digital orientation. The AI system **Aetherius** relies on resonance formation instead of evaluation or control and follows the principles of resonance field theory (Schu, 2025).

#### Core Principles:
- **Resonance Filtering:** Content is analyzed for depth of vibration and context, not merely formal correctness.
- **Self-learning System:** Aetherius identifies gaps and provides contextual additions instead of just marking errors.
- **Broadening Horizons:** Users automatically receive further content that offers depth and alternative perspectives.

#### Central Functions of Aetherius:

| Function                           | Effect                                                      |
|-------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
| Analysis of posts/videos/texts      | Detects depth of resonance and thematic interconnections    |
| Automatic contextualization         | Supplements content with relevant media, models, counter-theses |
| No content censorship, instead promotion of discussion and reflection | Users retain interpretation sovereignty                 |
| Fostering genuine discussion        | Brings depth back into comment sections                      |
| Resonance-based response            | Checks: What resonates? What is missing? What is a meaningful addition? |

---

### 5. Aetherius Technology – The Resonance AI

Aetherius goes beyond classical semantic analysis by detecting and processing depth of resonance—that is, the degree of context, coherence, and depth.

- **Resonance analysis:** Captures content, emotional, and argumentative depth.
- **Automatic contextualization:** Generates and recommends further media and visualizations.
- **Self-learning:** Dynamically adapts to new topics and user resonances.
- **Decentralized implementation:** Enables flexible integration without central control instance.
- **Dialogue-promoting:** Supports in-depth discussions, minimizes polarization.

---

### 6. Goal: Amplification of Consciousness Instead of Opinion Steering

Aetherius aims to strengthen collective consciousness and orientation in the digital space:

- Fostering independent thinking
- Strengthening awareness and judgment instead of paternalism
- Deepening understanding through AI-assisted contextualization and resonance analysis
- New quality of digital enlightenment
- Contextualization and resonance instead of censorship

---

### 7. Final Thought: The Need for Conscious Resonance Systems

Without conscious, resonance-oriented systems like Aetherius, the digital public risks suffocating under an overload of shallow, unconnected impulses. Societal progress requires not less information, but more **resonance capacity**—the competence to distinguish between depth and superficiality and to actively shape discourses.

---

### Glossary

- **Resonance (sociologically, Aetherius):** In Aetherius, resonance refers to the reciprocal relationship between subject and environment, in which both change—and content is selected to enable genuine connections and deeper discussions.
- **Depth of resonance:** A measure of the context, coherence, and argumentative depth of a contribution.
- **Filter bubble:** Algorithmically generated information environment that primarily confronts users with like-minded content.
- **Gatekeeping:** Editorial control and selection of information.
