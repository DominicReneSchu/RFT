# The Lost Connection – How True Love Disappears in the System of Utility

Modern society speaks much about love – yet it barely knows it anymore.  
What is considered a relationship today is often not a connection, but an arrangement.  
True resonance has become rare because its conditions have vanished:  
Time, depth, vulnerability, silence.

## Resonance Field Analysis: Love in the Context of Societal Structures

In a system shaped by utilitarian rationality and functionality, love is often reduced to its economic, social, or psychological usefulness. The actual resonance field of true love – a space of mutual openness, touchability, and inner movement – remains invisible.

- **Relationship as Contract**:  
  Partnerships are designed as social, legal, and economic arrangements. Individual resonance, the deep vibration between two beings, is overlaid by expectations, roles, and institutional frameworks.  
  The focus shifts from shared growth to compatibility and maximization of utility.

- **Lack of Time and Superficiality**:  
  Society fragments time through work, media consumption, and constant overstimulation. True encounter lacks leisure and mindfulness.  
  Love needs stillness, slowness, space for contemplation – conditions that are systemically excluded.

- **Vulnerability and Protective Mechanisms**:  
  Vulnerability is the prerequisite for resonance. Yet in the system of utility, openness is seen as a risk, not a resource.  
  Relationships are limited by fear of losing control and social judgement.  
  The resonance rule still applies: Even the unconnected are systemically included as a group.

- **Silence as Resonance Space**:  
  True love unfolds in silence. Yet the system creates noise – digital, social, mental.  
  Intimate silence, shared quiet, is misunderstood as emptiness, not as a space for deeper connection.
