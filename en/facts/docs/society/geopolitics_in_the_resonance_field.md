## 🧠 1. Entanglement instead of Dualism – Making Interdependent Actors Visible

Conflicts like the Ukraine war are usually staged in public discourse and media as confrontations between clearly defined camps – East vs. West, democracy vs. autocracy, good vs. evil. The dominant narrative suggests clear assignments and moral clarity. In reality, however, all actors – states, corporations, media houses, NGOs, shadow networks, digital platforms, economic and cultural interest groups as well as civil society – are entangled through multilayered, often invisible networks, dependencies, and interests.

Each group element assumes contradictory and multidimensional roles depending on the resonance axis, time window, or level of discourse. The same institution can simultaneously act as ally, adversary, mediator, beneficiary, or resonance amplifier. From a systemic perspective, even seemingly neutral or peripheral actors become part of the resonance field through self-inclusion and relational dynamics.

The following table exemplifies how key actors in the Ukraine war take on different, sometimes opposing system functions along various resonance axes:

| Actor                        | Official Role            | Systemic Double Role                                                    |
|------------------------------|-------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **NATO**                     | Defense alliance        | Escalation amplifier via troop presence and signaling policy            |
| **Western media**            | Information provider    | Discourse framer, instrument of emotionalization and legitimation       |
| **Ukrainian government**     | Victim nation           | Tactical resonance amplifier in the NATO corridor, resource channeler    |
| **Russian state**            | Aggressor               | Projection surface for system outsourcing and collective blame           |
| **Civil society**            | Sufferers               | Moral legitimation for escalation steps and sanction rhetoric            |
| **Shadow networks & oligarchs** | Invisible            | Resource shifting, sanction circumvention, influence logistics           |
| **Digital platforms**        | Neutral intermediary    | Filter bubble amplifier, segmentation, data aggregation for narratives   |
| **Corporations**             | Profiteers/victims      | Sanctions adapter, supply chain rerouter, resonance bridges between camps|

**Micro example:**  
A Western company supplies components for Russian energy projects despite embargoes – officially neutral, but systemically a bridge actor, resonance amplifier of hidden interests, and catalyst for network shifts.

**Systemic addition:**  
Even unnamed, peripheral, or seemingly uninvolved groups – such as international NGOs, data brokers, financial intermediaries, grassroots initiatives, fringe media, diaspora communities, or digital subcultures – are, through self-inclusion, data streams, narratives, and symbolic references, part of the resonance field and subtly influence the overall structure of the conflict.

## ♻️ 2. Making Feedback Loops Explicit

Every action, statement, and symbolic gesture of an involved party does not act linearly, but as part of a complex, circularly entangled resonance system that feeds back into the overall field. Media, politics, military, business, NGOs, digital platforms, shadow networks, data aggregators, civil society, and international organizations are all systemically coupled actors within it – even if not explicitly named.

A media report about a war crime is not merely information transfer, but a trigger of collective emotional resonance (fear, outrage, helplessness) that translates into societal approval for political decisions (e.g., arms deliveries, sanctions, mobilization). These decisions lead to material actions – further arms deliveries, intensification of sanctions, troop movements, economic redirections, digital campaigns – and generate new facts and images of victims. These images are picked up again by the media, emotionally intensified, algorithmically spread, and politically instrumentalized. This creates a self-reinforcing feedback loop, where every reaction further charges the system and accelerates the escalation spiral.

```text
[Media report: "Horrors of war"]
          ↓
[Emotional reaction: fear, anger]
          ↓
[Political approval: arms aid, sanctions, intervention]
          ↓
[Deliveries + escalation (military, economic, digital)]
          ↓
[New victim images, narratives, data streams → new media report]
          ↑
         (Loop)
```

**Example:**  
A viral video of a destroyed city triggers collective concern, which is algorithmically amplified and politically channeled – for example, as public pressure on decision-makers to approve new arms deliveries or tougher sanctions. This, in turn, leads to further escalation, new events, new images. The system amplifies itself.

**Systemic addition:**  
Even seemingly passive or peripheral groups – such as silent observers, comment sections, data analysts, international observers, peripheral economic actors, or digital subcultures – participate through their resonance, data, self-inclusion, or silence, stabilizing or amplifying the feedback loops. Every interaction, whether explicit, implicit, or algorithmically mediated, is part of the system mirror.

Each mechanism operates not in isolation, but in feedback and interaction with all other actors and group elements—states, NGOs, media, shadow networks, digital platforms, business, civil society, international organizations, data and analytics firms, social movements, lobby actors, algorithms, comment and subcultures. Even seemingly passive, marginal, or invisible groups are systemically involved via relations, data flows, narratives, and self-inclusion.

This legitimizes political sanctions, reinforces the enemy image, and channels approval—not only through media, but also via digital platforms, social movements, discourse actors, international institutions, and passive resonance groups.

**Systemic addition:**

Even peripheral, passive, or invisible actors (comment sections, data providers, diaspora, subcultures) stabilize and amplify these mechanisms through self-inclusion, silence, data flows, or symbolic reference.

In other conflicts—Iraq, Libya, Syria, Afghanistan, Yugoslavia—comparable mechanisms, narratives, escalation patterns, and group inclusions are evident. The labels, actors, and terms change, but the basic structure remains systemically invariant and operates across groups.

All explicit and implicit group elements—states, military alliances, intelligence services, NGOs, shadow networks, media consortia, corporations, digital platforms, civil society, international organizations, data aggregators, diaspora, subcultures, passive resonance groups—are embedded in cyclical repeat dynamics. **Systemic addition:**

## 📡 5. Making Fractal Micro-Macro Coupling Visible

The systemic polarization pattern of the Ukraine war—and comparable conflicts—does not only manifest on the macro level of geopolitical narratives, but permeates all social, media, economic, digital, and cultural domains down to the microstructures of individual communication and perception. The resonance logic is fractal: Every level—from international politics to private online comments, from NGO statements to everyday conversations, from economic decisions to algorithmically filtered newsfeeds—reproduces and modulates the same pattern of polarization, group assignment, enemy image cultivation, and discourse narrowing.

People who are critical of arms deliveries are casually dismissed as "Putin trolls" or "collaborators" on social media, in comment sections, and even in professional or family contexts. Simultaneously, the dominant macro narrative reads: "Russia rejects Western values—Europe is threatened—we must intervene." The attributions, exclusions, and interpretive frameworks on the micro level (online debates, discussion rounds, workplaces, classrooms, NGO meetings) are structurally identical to the mechanisms of geopolitical decision-making, media reporting, diplomatic rhetoric, and economic advocacy at the macro level.

**Example:**  
- Online: “Anyone against arms aid is a Putin troll”
- Political: “Russia threatens the freedom of Europe”
- Economy: “Companies that do not deliver endanger the West”
- NGO/initiatives: “Anyone mediating plays into Putin’s hands”
- Digital platforms: Algorithmic reduction of visibility for critical positions

**Insight:**  
The same resonance mechanism pervades everyday debates, institutional decisions, economic processes, media discourses, digital platform logic, and geopolitical narratives. Polarization is fractal and self-similar within the system—regardless of whether an actor is explicitly, implicitly, voluntarily, or peripherally involved. Every level feeds back into the overall field and stabilizes the system dynamic.

## 🧬 6. Metastructure: War as a Discharge Valve of the Debt Money System

Wars are not merely the culmination of geopolitical power interests, but fulfill a deep economic and systemic function within the resonance field of the debt money system. The entire network of explicit and implicit group elements—states, central banks, financial markets, commercial banks, governments, global corporations, shadow networks, the arms industry, international organizations, NGOs, media, civil society, data aggregators, digital platforms, passive investors and consumers—acts as an entangled field in the process of cyclically discharging and recreating debt, power, and capital binding.

Wars serve as a relief and discharge valve for over-indebtedness: They destroy existing assets and infrastructure, legitimize states of emergency, justify expropriations, and create space for new credit cycles. The material destruction becomes a resonance trigger for reconstruction programs, public investments, new debt, and the consolidation of economic and political control. The feedback loop between over-indebtedness, crisis, war, capital binding, reconstruction, and renewed indebtedness stabilizes the global debt money system in the resonance field of all participating groups.

```text
[Over-indebtedness system]
      ↓
[Inflation, distribution conflicts]
      ↓
[Crisis → legitimation of state of emergency]
      ↓
[War = expropriation + destruction + new loans]
      ↓
[Arms production → capital binding]
      ↓
[Reconstruction → new debt]
      ↑
  Cycle begins anew
```

**Example:**  
States take on new loans for arms deliveries, arms companies and suppliers profit, banks refinance the chains, infrastructure is destroyed, international organizations and financial markets set up reconstruction programs, and reconstruction indebts societies once again. Media, digital platforms, and political narratives legitimize the state of emergency. The cycle sustains itself through the systemic coupling of all explicit and implicit elements of resonance.

**Systemic addition:**  
Even seemingly peripheral or passive groups—such as consumers, pension funds, municipalities, diaspora, shadow banks, data brokers, technical infrastructure operators, digital subcultures—are part of this discharge and reproduction cycle of the debt money system through self-inclusion, data streams, capital participation, tax revenues, or symbolic reference.

## 🧹 7. Resonance Field Synthesis as Closing Structure

All previously described dynamics, groups, mechanisms, and feedback loops operate together within the resonance field of the geopolitical system. No actor, no opposition, no peripheral or seemingly passive group is left out—whether by explicit actions, narrative references, data flows, symbolic self-inclusion, or relational effects. All group elements—states, governments, media, military, arms industry, financial power, NGOs, digital platforms, shadow networks, population, international organizations, data brokers, subcultures, discourse actors, dissenters, passive observers—are systemically interwoven and embedded in cyclical feedback processes.

The apparent disruption of "war" is, from a systemic perspective, not an exception but a discharge and stabilization of the overall system. The field remains closed upon itself; every tension is redirected, every opposition integrated or neutralized—until the next accumulation and discharge.


**Insight:**  
The resonance field is always comprehensive: Every explicit and implicit group affiliation, every opposition, every passively or actively resonating instance stabilizes, amplifies, or modulates the system dynamics. War is not a breakdown, but a discharge; peace is not a return to “normality,” but an awakening and dissolution of systemically distorted resonance. Only systemic awareness—making all groups, relations, and mechanisms transparent—opens the path to genuine transformation and sustainable cooperation, beyond dualism, enemy images, and repetition compulsion.
