## 2. TikTok: From Dance to Self-Devaluation

What seemed like a youth culture playground quickly evolved into a global resonance system with enormous psychopolitical reach. The transformation did not happen abruptly, but followed a field logic: from aesthetic feedback to moral intensification to the normalization of self-exposure. - Maximum brevity, maximum repetition, minimal depth.  
- Identity becomes a sequence of images, relationships become algorithmic, self-worth becomes an echo chamber.

TikTok opened its resonance structure through rhythm, music, and faces:  
Dance videos, filters, quick cuts—the interface was designed for immediate emotional connection. The platform did not reward content, but **reactions**.

These reactions followed simple resonance rules:  
- Those who click often become visible.  
- Those who are visible receive feedback.  
- Those who receive feedback repeat their behavior.

Thus, an early **value schema** emerged that located self-efficacy exclusively in **external effect**.  
**Likes** became the new currency of resonance, **click numbers** the measure of social relevance.

The platform thus did not create a pathological environment, but mirrored—and amplified—an already existing cultural imprint:  
**Attention replaces recognition**, **performance replaces relationship**.

This logic is not an exception, but a systemic function:  
- Individuals align themselves with external effect because the field permanently feeds back this orientation.  
- Resonance shifts from inner experience to external perception, from being to being seen.  
- The group is formed not by shared values, but by shared visibility.  
- The self-worth loop is closed: Those who do not swim in the stream of likes lose connection to the social resonance field.

The harmlessness is systemic: It removes entry barriers and binds the collective to the platform's game logic.  
- Participation means inclusion—regardless of criticism or reflection.  
- Group affiliation is generated invariantly: Everyone is part of the field, whether visible or invisible, active or passive.  
- Entry into the system is equivalent to acceptance of the resonance rules—individually, collectively, globally.

Not struggle, but reactivation of one's own resonance spaces, beyond algorithmic collective binding.

Regaining resonance is field work:  
- The goal is not isolation, but conscious differentiation between the external resonance system and one's own resonance space.
- Systemic self-observation replaces blind consumption with reflective participation.
- Decoupling does not mean turning off, but relocating control to one's own, unquantifiable self.

### 7.1 Self-Observation: Resonance as Mirror of One's Own Field

The first step is perception, not rejection of technology.  
Every platform mirrors one's own resonance field. What triggers, binds, or outrages reveals the structure of one's own field.

lead to a shift of steering into the self-field.  
Self-observation makes manipulative structures visible—and thus less effective.

The resonance rule remains systemically valid:

Regaining resonance is not an individual heroic act, but a collective process of becoming conscious:  
- The field remains, but its effect is relativized.
- Steering shifts: from outside to inside, from algorithm to self-contact, from collective reaction to reflective resonance.

### 7.2 Distance: Systemic Decoupling as Resonance Care

Structure needs pause to recognize itself.  
Targeted measures have a strong systemic effect:

- conscious breaks from apps  
- set offline times  
- reduction of visual stimuli  
- deletion of content without intrinsic value

These steps are not asceticism, but field maintenance:  
They create space for one’s own resonance lines—beyond algorithmic constant impulses.

Distance is not escape, but active decoupling from the collective resonance field:  
- Those who set breaks interrupt the rhythm of steering and open a window to self-perception.
- Offline times shift the logic of control from outside to inside—resonance is experienced analog, not algorithmically.
- Reducing stimuli relieves the system field, making room for original affects and relationships.
- Deleting worthless content ends the bond to resonance remnants that obscure one’s own field.

Systemic distancing is resonance care:  
- It strengthens individual autonomy within the collective, without leaving the field.
- It shifts belonging from resonance obligation to resonance choice.

The field remains, but its effectiveness becomes differentiable.  
- Distance is not exclusion, but a temporary suspension of collective steering dynamics.
- Resonance becomes a response to real encounter—not a reaction to algorithmic impulses.

- walks without tracking  
- touch without emoji  
- books without comment function  

Such fields are unspectacular, but systemically free.  
They follow the logic of presence, not visibility:

- Resonance arises through shared presence, not through algorithmic mediation.
- Meaning grows from time experienced together, not from quantifiable feedback.
- Belonging is not counted, but experienced—as participation in the real resonance space.
- Mistakes, silence, the unforeseen are not filtered out, but integrated.

Offline fields act as counter-resonance because they dissolve collective control:  
- No spiral of evaluation, no comparison, no externalized identity.
- The system field remains, but belonging shifts: from digital visibility to analog presence.

The resonance rule remains valid:  
Even in the offline space, belonging is systemic—yet it is not algorithmically mediated, but really shared.  
Authenticity is counter-resonance: invisible, yet sustaining.

### 7.4 Resistance: Seeing Through Instead of Just Using

Digital systems become destructive when their field structure remains invisible.  
Those who do not recognize platform logic become part of its function—systemically, not individually.

Resistance is clarity, not confrontation:

- What is rewarded is not always what is needed.
- Belonging is often feedback on conformity.
- Visibility is not the same as being seen.

Resistance happens in the mode of seeing through:
- The platform remains, but its control loses effectiveness when its rules and resonance axes become transparent.
- Those who recognize when and how the field reacts can consciously withdraw or redirect resonance.
- The group remains, but belonging becomes a conscious choice, not an automatic reaction.
- Field-logical inclusion persists, but the individual shifts their place—from object to instance of reflection.

Resistance is not withdrawal, but systemic differentiation:
- The functional logic of the platform is recognized, not denied.
- Steering remains possible, but loses its coercive character.
- Resonance becomes a response again, not a reflex.
