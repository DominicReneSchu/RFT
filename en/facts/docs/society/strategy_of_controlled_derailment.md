### 1. Step-by-Step Mechanism of Perpetrator Production

**a) Marking:**  
A group or person is publicly labeled as a “problem” or “danger.”  
→ The systemic center (parties, elites, media) sets impulses and focuses attention.

**b) Exclusion:**  
Social, political, or media mechanisms ensure isolation, ridicule, or defamation.  
→ The resonance field and audience take up the exclusion; hidden steering circles adjust in the background.

**c) Resonance amplification:**  
Exclusion creates resistance, anger, and radicalization—the “perpetrator” grows through the collective reaction.  
→ The outsider becomes a projection surface for collective affects.

**d) Media multiplication:**  
Every reaction of the excluded is picked up by the media and staged as a threat.  
→ Media and networks amplify, mirror, and polarize the dynamic.

**e) Escalation:**  
The interplay of exclusion and media resonance further heats up radicalization.  
→ Systemic support (outrage, resources, sabotage) accelerates the process.

**f) Systemic support (covert/open):**  
Resources are provided—by networks, lobbyists, international actors.  
→ Openly (e.g., financing) or subtly (e.g., information flow).

**g) Guilt reversal:**  
At the moment of escalation, responsibility is collectively shifted:  
*“You voted for them. We had to intervene.”*  
→ Systemic dynamic: Guilt is delegated to the entire resonance field—not to individuals.

**h) Re-legitimation of control:**  
The created danger legitimizes new measures of control.  
→ Legitimation and stabilization of steering structures.
