## Geopolitical Distrust and the Illusion of Diplomatic Order

---

### Abstract

The cancelled summit between Russia and Ukraine, as well as debates around international arrest warrants, reveal: Geopolitical distrust undermines international institutions and blocks sustainable peace processes. This article explains why only a conscious coupling with spiritual principles and resonance fields can enable global stability.

---

### 1. Introduction and Case Description

In May 2025, a planned meeting between Russian President Vladimir Putin and Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky in Istanbul was cancelled at short notice. Media and experts discuss security concerns and the possibility of Putin’s arrest by the International Criminal Court (ICC) as reasons.  
At the same time, German Chancellor Friedrich Merz’s statement that a visit by Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu to Germany would be possible despite an ICC arrest warrant sparked debates about the enforceability of international law.

These incidents raise the question of how binding and effective international norms truly are when political leaders can bypass or ignore them (Ambos, 2013; Schabas, 2017).

---

### 2. Scientific Context

The enforcement of international norms depends not only on legal mechanisms, but also on political and societal resonances (Luhmann, 1997; Koskenniemi, 2002). International institutions like the ICC are effective especially when their principles are anchored in collective consciousness (Zürn, 2018).

Resonance field theory (Schu, 2025) adds: Sustainable order does not arise from external compulsion, but from the synchronization of spiritual principles and active fields of consciousness. True order is based on inner coherence, not on demonstrations of power. The concept of resonance here connects to sociological perspectives that see resonance as the foundation of successful world relations (Rosa, 2016).

---

### 3. Distrust vs. Spiritual Principles – Dynamics of Geopolitical Fields

Distrust shapes many current conflicts. It prevents open negotiations and fosters control and manipulation. Meetings are cancelled because the risk is perceived as too high—trust is lacking.

Practically, resonance field theory emphasizes:  
When negotiators build on spiritual principles such as transparency, self-reflection, and mutual respect, resonance and trust arise. For example: In local peace processes, open dialogue formats following Lederach (1997) foster lasting understanding, because they create trust and resonance.

---

### 4. Paths Out of Deadlock – Active Resonance Instead of Competition

Leaders must actively free themselves from patterns of competition and control. They should rely on spiritual principles and consciously enable resonance—through transparent communication, active listening, and self-reflection.  
Resonance field theory (Schu, 2025) shows: Those who synchronize fields of consciousness create sustainable, systemic stability. In practice, this can be achieved through trust-building and shared values.

Peace and conflict research confirms: Trust, communication, and identity-building are central foundations of effective orders (Lederach, 1997; Mitchell, 2023).

---

### 5. Summary

Geopolitical distrust weakens international institutions and prevents sustainable peace. Only a transformation of mindset, trust, and resonance capacity can create global stability.  
**Sustainable peace arises in minds, not just at summits.**
