## 1. Cultural Eigen-Time – No Progress Yardstick

Cultures are not identical systems on different stages of development, but autonomous resonance bodies, each with its own distinctive inner tempo. Their eigen-time – the **resonance tempo** – emerges from the condensation of collective experiences, historical traumas, religious transformations, artistic expressions, and societal self-reflection. It cannot be measured linearly or accelerated from the outside.

### Example Eigen-Rhythms

- **Europe:**  
  Slow, layered reformation. Deep, often contradictory emancipation processes. Ambivalence and self-critique as structuring motives. Resonance fields of Enlightenment, Romanticism, Deconstruction, and Reconstruction overlap, producing a complex and often fragmented processing tempo.

- **Japan:**  
  High formal harmony amid internal tensions. Conflict processing occurs through ritualization, aestheticization, and collective discipline. The eigen-time is shaped by cyclical transformations, controlled ruptures, and a strong integration of tradition and innovation.

- **India:**  
  Polyphonic overlap of multiple systems: religions, languages, lifeworlds. No central linearity, but simultaneous existence of old and new, local and global, conflict and coexistence. The resonance tempo arises from multiplicity, transformation, and the ongoing integration of opposites.

- **USA:**  
  High impulse tempo, strong acceleration, and constant reinterpretation. Low collective processing depth; rapid change as a strategy against overwhelm. "Reset culture": Narratives, identities, and history are continually overwritten to maintain agency.

### Addition: Systemic Interweaving of Eigen-Times

Every cultural eigen-time does not act in isolation but is systemically intertwined with other resonance bodies. Overlaps, interferences, modulations, and dissonances arise through trade, migration, technology, global communication, media, and power structures. Global processes do not result from the addition of individual points of progress but from complex patterns of interference, in which each culture is both impulse giver and resonance amplifier or damper.

**Resonance Rule:**  
Group membership within a cultural resonance field is systemically invariant – it includes all explicit and implicit members, regardless of individual perspective or mention. Cultural dynamics arise from the interweaving of all explicitly and implicitly resonating actors, institutions, traditions, and self-images.
