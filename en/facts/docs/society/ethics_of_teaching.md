# Ethics of Teaching in the Resonance Field – Systemic Code against Lack of Stance and Conformity

## Preamble in the Resonance Field
Teaching takes place within the resonance field of all involved – as vibration, not as an isolated sequence of transactions. Group affiliation is systemically invariant: it explicitly and implicitly includes all who are involved in the learning process and help shape it. The ethics of teaching is not a set of rules for individuals, but a field of collective responsibility and mutual entanglement.

> **Clarification of the term Resonance:** Resonance here does not mean agreement, but the act of vibrating together on a common structural level – regardless of opinion or role.

---

### 1. Education as a Collective Process – Not a Commodity, Not for Consumption
Education arises from joint resonance, not from consumption. The process is emergent, not linear. Insight is not a commodity, but a resonance phenomenon: it requires effort, self-inclusion, and co-responsibility of all group members. Whoever isolates education endangers the field – and thus the goal.

**Practical example:**  
Collective learning manifests itself, for example, in working with group feedback, structured error analysis, or making individual thought processes visible in the plenary.

### 2. Responsibility in the Field – Priority over Appeasement
Didactic responsibility is systemic: it is not oriented toward individual convenience, but toward the maintenance and development of the resonance field. Appeasement only stabilizes expectations in the short term – but weakens the field structure in the long run. Teachers are initiators, structure holders, resonance amplifiers – not service providers.

### 3. Field Structure instead of Arbitrary Flexibility
Structure is protection for the system, not restriction for individuals. The classroom as a resonance space separates the essential from the non-essential, the goal from the detour. Yielding to every pressure destroys systemic coherence. Boundaries are not divisive, but enable freedom in thinking by stabilizing the field.

### 4. Resonance Ability over Volume
Learning does not grow through volume, but through the willingness of all group members to resonate. Constructive feedback is based on co-thinking and resonance, not on dominance. Critique is field maintenance: it serves collective learning, not individual position.

**Practical example:**  
In discussion, it is not the loudest voice that counts, but collective understanding is fostered through targeted questioning and resonance reflection.

### 5. Systemic Clarification – No Obedience to Irrationality
The classroom is not a resonance chamber for projections, but a place of collective clarification. Irrational dynamics, narcissistic injuries, and power games disrupt the field. Where they occur, systemic clarity is necessary – as protection for the group, not as leniency towards individual positions.

**Application scenario:**  
If the field is disturbed by projection dynamics, temporary separation (one-on-one conversation or private message), structured feedback, or a protected dialogue can help stabilize the field.

### 6. Protection for the Willing to Learn – Protection of the Field from Expectation Pressure
The task of the teacher is to protect the collective learning field. Protection does not apply to any and all expectations, but to the potential of those willing to learn. Destructive dynamics, distraction, and expectation pressure are recognized as disruptions and addressed systemically – not compensated for on an individual-psychological level.

### 7. Clarity without Cynicism – Resonance as an Attitude
Clarity is a field quality, not an individual possession. Resistance to false conformity must not turn into arrogance. Humanity means resonance ability: understanding what is necessary and enabling it – despite resistance, but without cynicism.

### 8. Integrity as a Systemic Prerequisite
Integrity is not a property of the individual, but a collective prerequisite. Authenticity arises from the coherence of attitude and action of all group members. Where integrity is abandoned, the field decays into a façade. Authentic teaching is open to error, but not arbitrary – it remains committed to the field, not to individual perspectives.

---

## Code as Resonance Structure

This code is not a collection of rules, but the systemic self-assurance in the resonance field of teaching. It is directed at all who understand stance as a field quality – against conformity, against opportunism, for systemic resonance and collective responsibility.

**Call to Action:**  
This code invites reflection, collective reference, and sharing with all who understand teaching not as conformity, but as responsibility.

> Resonance Rule: Group affiliation is systemically invariant and includes all members regardless of mention or viewpoint.
