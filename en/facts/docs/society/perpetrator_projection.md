## Perpetrator Projection in the Guise of Anti-Fascism

**Systemic Dynamics of Narcissistic Power in Moral Language**

---

### 1. Introduction: The Mask of Morality

Not everyone who speaks of evil is fighting it. Evil reveals itself less in appearance than in structure. Authoritarian systems learn to legitimize themselves not through open violence but through moral or linguistic rhetoric. Those who stage themselves as fighters against darkness become immune to their own shadow. Perpetrator projection becomes a cloak of invisibility – the destructive potential appears only externally. The resonance rule applies: Even what is morally excluded remains systemically part of the field.

---

### 2. Structural Features of Narcissistic Systems

* **Projection and Reversal:** The undesirable is split off, fought against, and located outside. Projection serves self-purification – guilt and danger always exist with the “others.” Systemically, what is split off remains effective in the resonance field.
* **Language Regulation and Pressure to Conform:** Truth is secondary to formal alignment. Those who do not choose the “right” words or pronouns fall under suspicion – regardless of the content of their statements. Belonging is defined by language, not arguments. Language serves as a marker: refusal to adopt the new language marks one as an outsider. In authoritarian systems, linguistic conformity is an early and revealing instrument of rule. In Hitler’s time, the salute was used to test belonging – today, it is other linguistic signals.
* **Gaslighting:** The perceptions of others are systematically distorted. Critical thoughts and experiences are reinterpreted, victims declared perpetrators, doubts seen as attacks. The field loses shared reality.
* **Moral Self-Immunization:** Criticism is not answered argumentatively but morally devalued. Dissenters are labeled as inhumane, dangerous, outside the permissible discourse.
* **Systemic Loss of Resonance:** Feedback between individual and group breaks down – learning and development are prevented. The group becomes static, and shadows continue to work systemically.

---

### 3. Anti-Fascism as a Narcissistic Cover

Anti-fascism is historically necessary and remains systemically relevant. But authoritarian dynamics can use its terms:

* The concept of fascism is expanded and loses analytical sharpness.
* Anti-fascist rhetoric can serve as a projection surface: those with dissenting opinions are labeled “right-wing.” One’s own actions remain untouched.
* Plurality and differentiated discourse are replaced by binary friend-enemy thinking. The group sees itself as a bulwark, while in reality it loses resonance and tolerance for ambiguity.
* Linguistic markers are introduced as tests: those who do not participate are excluded. Language itself creates the boundary – not arguments, but correct speech determines belonging.

Thus, a system can become totalitarian while presenting itself as a defender of openness and diversity. The resonance rule applies: what is excluded remains part of the field and continues to act as a shadow.

### 5. Paths to Systemic Resolution

* **Self-inclusion instead of Enemy Maintenance:** The group recognizes itself as a resonance field that also integrates its own shadows and mistakes. Development becomes possible when the foreign is recognized as part of oneself.
* **Mirror Work:** Strangeness and irritation are opportunities for self-knowledge. The group asks: What in me resonates with that which I reject?
* **Resonance Ethics:** Not the pure attitude or correct confession counts, but the quality of relationship and the ability to endure and productively transform tension and difference.
* **Systemic Dialogue:** Contradiction is seen as a chance to broaden perspectives, not as a threat.
* **Linguistic Resonance:** The diversity of language is recognized as a resource – not as a threat. Linguistic difference generates insight, not exclusion.

The alternative to projection is not agreement but presence: In the resonance field, difference is used as a resource. The resonance rule guarantees: every element remains part of the whole, regardless of evaluation, language, or position.

### 6. Symbolic Self-Localization as a Deception Strategy

Totalitarian and manipulative systems disguise their exercise of power not only with morality but also with symbolic identification and language:

* They style themselves as part of the “common people,” as the voice of the oppressed, as protectors of the people.
* **Hitler’s Self-Presentation:** Frontline soldier, friend of workers, nature lover – in reality, leader of a destructive, ideologically driven apparatus of annihilation.
* **Modern Party and State Rhetoric:** Terms such as social justice, solidarity, peace, or the obligation to certain forms of speech are used as rhetorical masks. In practice: militarization, exclusion of critical voices, management of scarcity and insecurity.
* **Linguistic Belonging as Power Instrument:** Those who do not adopt the prescribed language are excluded – language becomes a filter of belonging and a tool of control.

What appears as help becomes subtle management and perpetuation of powerlessness:

* Low-income earners are pacified by redistribution, not empowered.
* War is labeled as a protective measure, but creates new dependencies and destroys habitats.
* Democracy and diversity are invoked symbolically, but in practice undermined by language rules, secret negotiations, networks, and intransparency.

**Resonance is lacking where symbolic identification and linguistic uniformity replace reality.**
In the end stands disappointment – as the end of deception, as an invitation to new clarity. The resonance rule operates: systemic belonging remains, even when identification is illusory and language exclusive.

### 6a. The Role of Observers and Bystanders – Resonance Field Instead of Individualized Guilt

Anti-fascist perpetrator projection unfolds its effect not only through obvious actors, but through the co-resonance of the entire group. The resonance rule shows: Not only the loud drivers, but also silent followers, the silent, and conformists are systemically part of the process – regardless of intention or explicit action.

* **Conformism as a Resonance Phenomenon:** Systemic dynamics are stabilized by silent consent and passive following. Shifting responsibility to individuals obscures the fact that the entire field – even the seemingly uninvolved – resonates and sustains the system.
* **Invisible Complicity:** Those who tolerate dissenting voices but do not support them become part of the exclusion dynamic. Guilt individualizes, the resonance field integrates: responsibility is shared, even if it is differently visible.
* **Systemic Blindness:** Focusing on perpetrators and “guilty” parties prevents recognition of one’s own involvement. The resonance field makes clear: every passive affirmation, every looking away, every adaptation to language rules acts as an amplifier of group dynamics.
* **Transformation through Self-Observation:** Self-knowledge as a systemic corrective: The question is not “Who is guilty?”, but: Where, how, and why do I resonate (with this)? What collective patterns do I perpetuate by not intervening?

## Resonance Rule: The Systemic Resolution of the System Game

**Those who exclude, reinforce. Those who project, bind. Those who fight, confirm.**
The resonance rule breaks the game of guilt and perpetrator projection by revealing a fundamental insight: **Group belonging is systemically invariant.** It includes **all parts** – even those that are repressed, denied, labeled as “evil.” Attempts to exclude unwanted parts not only fail to remove their belonging, but **reinforce them through exclusion**.

---

### 1. The End of the System Game

The traditional power game is based on fragmentation:

* **Perpetrators are externalized.**
* **Guilt is shifted.**
* **Own shadows are projected.**
  Yet within a resonance field: **Every exclusion generates feedback.**
  The *fought against* is unconsciously bound and stabilized by projection.

> **Those who exclude lose control – not power. Power remains in the resonance field; control breaks on it.**

---

### 2. Responsibility Instead of Guilt

The resonance rule decouples responsibility from moral guilt:

* It requires no confession of guilt, but systemic **self-inclusion**.
* Responsibility means recognizing *even the unwanted parts* as belonging to the group.
* Change is not brought by moral condemnation, but by **structural transparency**.

---

### 3. Transformation Through Recognition

Change begins when the “other” is no longer excluded.
Because:

* **It belongs.**
* **It is already there.**
* **It resonates in the field.**

> “It is not exclusion that transforms the field, but acknowledgment of its full structure.”

---

### 4. Consequence: Paradigm Shift

This insight is not an ethic – it is a **systemic inevitability**:

* No power game works once the whole is visible.
* No perpetrator image stands when all parts are mirrored in the field.
* No manipulation remains effective when projection is recognized as feedback.

---

**The resonance rule not only ends the game – it renders it obsolete.**
For it reveals: *What is excluded returns stronger – until it is acknowledged.*

This is the systemic lever that changes the world.

---

### 7. Conclusion: Recognize Instead of Fight

Fighting evil often leads to its reproduction – if the structure remains unchanged. The resonance field offers an alternative: those who recognize no longer have to fight, but can integrate and dissolve dynamics.

* The future belongs not to those who see themselves as better, but to those who recognize themselves in the other and can transform tension – without mask, without projection, without defense.
* The resonance rule remains systemically invariant: group membership encompasses every element, regardless of language, viewpoint, or evaluation.

#### Glossary

* **Resonance Field:** A system in which all elements – explicit and implicit – interact with each other.
