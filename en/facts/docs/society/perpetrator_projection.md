## Perpetrator Projection in the Guise of Antifascism

**Systemic Dynamics of Narcissistic Power in Moral Language**

---

### 1. Introduction: The Mask of Morality

Not everyone who speaks of evil is actually fighting it. Evil reveals itself less in appearance than in structure. Authoritarian systems learn to legitimize themselves not through open violence, but through moral or linguistic rhetoric. Those who stage themselves as fighters against darkness become numb to their own shadow. Perpetrator projection becomes a cloak of invisibility—the destructive potential appears only on the outside. The resonance rule applies: Even what is morally excluded remains systemically part of the field.

**Deepening and Systemic Expansion:**

Perpetrator projection in the context of antifascism operates as a societal resonance mechanism that shifts power and responsibility. When groups or institutions assume the role of moral authority, they externalize shortcomings, aggression, and destructive impulses onto a defined outside—“the others”, “the right-wing”, “the threats”. This projection seems to relieve the internal field but actually creates a fatal shadow integration: Everything unrecognized or unacknowledged internally seeks a counterpart externally.

Moral language serves as a resonance amplifier. Terms like “tolerance,” “open-mindedness,” and “defense of democracy” become symbolic currencies with which belonging and legitimacy in the system are acquired. The stronger the public staging of good, the more invisible the systemic complicity with mechanisms of exclusion, control, and blame assignment.

Perpetrator projection is not an individual failure but a collective pattern. The antifascist discourse thus becomes a mirror field: Those who place themselves exclusively on the side of light evade self-reflection and immunize themselves against criticism. The result: Not fascism as a structure is fought, but its symbolic representatives. The actual system dynamics remain and reproduce—just on another level, with reversed signs.

Within the resonance field of this dynamic, stable lines of division emerge: The community of the “good” against the “evil”, with every unrecognized shadow further charging the field. The resonance rule remains valid: Even the excluded—the supposed perpetrator—remains systemically part of the whole field and has an effect on it. Moral immunization creates blindness to one's own destructive potentials and perpetuates precisely those structures one believes to overcome.

---

### 2. Structural Characteristics of Narcissistic Systems

Within the resonance field of narcissistic power systems—regardless of their political or moral guise—recurring structural features can be identified, which encompass the entire field, not just individual actors. Each of these dynamics is part of an overarching pattern in which the system immunizes itself and systematically outsources its shadows. The resonance rule holds: What is not integrated remains effective in the overall field.

**Projection and Reversal:**  
What cannot be allowed or integrated in one's own field—fear, guilt, aggression, doubt—is split off and fought externally. Projection serves ongoing self-cleansing: guilt and danger exist only “out there,” while one's own system stages itself as pure and moral. Yet what is split off remains effective in the resonance field, unconsciously influencing the thinking, feeling, and behavior of the group. Collective projection thus creates a dynamic shadow landscape in which the repressed returns as an enemy image.

**Language Regulation and Pressure to Conform:**  
Not authenticity or truth, but formal alignment with prevailing modes of speech determines belonging. Language becomes both marker and test: Those who do not adopt the “correct” words, pronouns, or narratives lose group connection. Belonging is defined by mastery and acceptance of new language rules, not by arguments or substance. What was once the “German Greeting” as a test of belonging is today replaced by linguistic signals that indicate allegiance and loyalty. Those who resist are isolated, devalued, or rendered invisible. Language itself becomes an instrument of domination and a testing ground for systemic loyalty.

**Gaslighting:**  
The perception of individuals is systematically destabilized. Criticism, doubt, personal experience—everything can be reinterpreted: Victims are declared perpetrators, criticism is interpreted as aggression, dissenting perception is pathologized. In this way, the field loses a shared sense of reality; resonance and trust break down. The group remains only apparently united, while inner insecurity and mistrust grow. Gaslighting is not an individual phenomenon, but a collective process of reality manipulation.

**Moral Self-Immunization:**  
Arguments are no longer examined on substance, but reduced to their moral status. Those who disagree are not considered worthy of discussion, but are labeled inhuman, dangerous, “right-wing”, or “unsolidaric”. Moral immunization protects the system from self-reflection and prevents development. Criticism becomes proof of the system’s own moral superiority. Thus, the narcissistic self-image remains stable while dissenting voices are systematically excluded.

**Systemic Loss of Resonance:**  
The feedback loop between individual and group—the interplay of feedback, criticism, and development—breaks down. The group becomes rigid, learning processes no longer take place. Shadows and repressed elements continue to have unconscious effects, reinforcing division and stagnation. The system immunizes itself against change and loses its vitality. The resonance rule demonstrates: What is not integrated remains as a disturbance in the overall field and seeks new forms of expression—in divisions, projections, and collective crises.

---

In the resonance field of narcissistic systems, these structures are not additive but interwoven: projection generates language regulation, gaslighting intensifies resonance loss, and moral self-immunization stabilizes the entire system. The field thus becomes a closed circuit—morally outward, authoritarian inward, and in its entirety incapable of integrating its own shadow.

### 3. Antifascism as Narcissistic Camouflage

Antifascism is historically necessary and remains relevant as a systemic corrective. Yet any moral position can be co-opted by authoritarian dynamics—especially when it becomes the unquestioned core of a group’s identity. In the resonance field of societal power shifts, antifascism itself becomes a projection space for shadow aspects that the system is unwilling to integrate.

* The term "fascism" becomes unbounded: Originally analytic and historically precise, it turns into a universal battle word within the narcissistic system. Any deviation, doubt, or criticism can now be subsumed under the label "right-wing", "fascistoid", or "reactionary". The analytical sharpness of the term is lost—replaced by a moral ban that prevents differentiation.

* Antifascist rhetoric serves as a projection surface: Destructive potential is shifted outward. Anyone deviating from the official group consensus is marked as "right-wing", regardless of their actual positions. One’s own actions remain exempt from reflection; moral immunity protects against self-criticism. Exclusion becomes a virtue, projection the system’s logic.

* Plurality and differentiated discourse erode: The field becomes binary, friend-foe logic replaces tolerance for ambiguity. The group presents itself as a bulwark against evil, while in reality it loses resonance and multi-perspective openness. Ambivalence, uncertainty, contradiction—all are devalued as potential dangers. The field hardens, and the Other is systematically excluded.

* Linguistic markers are introduced as belonging tests: Correct language replaces substantive argument. Those who refuse the new codes are excluded not for their ideas, but for their speech behavior. Language itself creates the boundary; the field of belonging is defined by communication signals, not discussion.

Thus, a system can become totalitarian and exclusionary while presenting itself outwardly as a defender of diversity and openness. The resonance rule persists: Everything excluded remains systemically part of the field—as a shadow, as an unconscious force, as the potential return of the repressed. The moral self-image does not protect against the structural repetition of what is fought—it merely shifts the levels and codes of power.

### 5. Paths to Systemic Resolution

In the complete resonance field of narcissistic and projective dynamics, a perspective of integration and evolution emerges beyond exclusion and enemy images—one that encompasses all systemic elements, both explicit and implicit, in their mutual interplay.

* **Self-Inclusion Instead of Enemy-Making:**  
  The group understands itself as an open resonance field, in which shadows, irritations, and mistakes are integrated as part of the self. Development arises when what is foreign or rejected is recognized as a mirror of inner dynamics. The boundary between inside and outside becomes permeable, and the shadows lose their destructive power.

* **Mirror Work:**  
  Otherness, contradiction, and irritation are not repelled but used as opportunities for self-knowledge. The central question becomes: What in me, in us, resonates with what we reject in others? The group recognizes its own parts in the counterpart—resonance replaces projection.

* **Resonance Ethics:**  
  The focus is not on the “right” confession or morally flawless attitude, but on the quality of relationship: the ability to hold tension, ambiguity, and difference productively. Resonance ethics means seeing relationship and plurality as a resource, not a threat.

* **Systemic Dialogue:**  
  Contradiction, criticism, and difference are welcomed as opportunities to expand collective perspectives. Dialogue is not used for homogenization but as an opening for new insights. In this way, collective learning emerges within the resonance field.

* **Linguistic Resonance:**  
  The diversity of language—dialects, codes, modes of expression—is recognized as a potential for insight and development. Linguistic difference generates new perspectives and opens spaces for integration rather than sharpening exclusion.

The alternative to perpetrator projection is not mere agreement, but present, conscious participation in the resonance field: Difference is used as a resource, not seen as a threat. The resonance rule remains systemically inviolable: Every element—every position, every voice, every irritation—remains part of the whole, regardless of evaluation, narrative, or language. Only through awareness of this systemic inclusion does true transformation become possible.

### 6. Symbolic Self-Localization as a Deception Strategy

Totalitarian and manipulative systems hide their exercise of power not only through moral elevation, but also through targeted symbolic identification and linguistic staging. Affiliation with “the people,” “justice,” or an imagined victim role becomes a systemic cloak legitimizing domination and control.

* Staging as representatives of “the common people,” as advocates for the oppressed, or as protectors of the common good serves emotional bonding and makes power difficult to challenge. Real power relations are thus concealed systemically and translated into symbolic closeness to the base.

* **Hitler’s Self-Portrayal:**  
  The myth of the simple front-line soldier, friend of workers, and lover of nature concealed the leadership role in a destructive, ideologically charged apparatus of power. Identification with archetypal images (hero, friend, nature) served as a collective projection surface—the destructive system logic remained invisible, and resonance at the symbolic level became a function of domination.

* **Modern Party and State Rhetoric:**  
  Terms like social justice, solidarity, peace, or the obligation to certain forms of speech function as rhetorical masks. They create a feeling of belonging and moral legitimacy, while in practice, militarization, exclusion of critical voices, management of scarcity, and perpetuation of insecurity are pursued. Symbolic identification replaces real participation and conceals the underlying matrix of power.

* **Linguistic Belonging as a Tool of Power:**  
  Language becomes a filter: Those who do not adopt prescribed terms, narratives, or modes of speech lose social connection. Language regulations create belonging and exclusion at the same time—control shifts from the level of action to the symbolic and communicative level.

What is declared as help, protection, or solidarity often in reality serves the subtle management of powerlessness and dependency:

* Low earners are pacified through symbolic redistribution but are not enabled for genuine self-determination.
* War and intervention are labeled as protective measures but create new dependencies, insecurities, and destroy real living spaces.
* Democracy and diversity are symbolically invoked—yet in practice are undermined by language regulations, secret networks, and systemic intransparency.

**Resonance is absent wherever symbolic identification and linguistic uniformity replace reality.**  
In the end, disappointment stands—as the end of deception and as an invitation to new clarity and systemic self-reflection. The resonance rule continues to operate: Even when identification is illusory and language becomes exclusive, the systemic belonging of all elements remains. True transformation begins when the field recognizes the difference between symbol and substance.

### 6a. The Role of Observers and Followers – Resonance Field Instead of Individualized Guilt

Antifascist perpetrator projection unfolds its systemic effect not only through the obvious actors but through the co-resonance and reverberation of the entire group. The resonance rule shows: Not only the loud drivers, but also silent followers, the quiet, and conformists are integral parts of the overall process—regardless of intention or explicit action.

* **Conformity as a Resonance Phenomenon:**  
  Systemic dynamics are stabilized by silent consent, passive compliance, and the willingness not to stand out. Individualizing guilt onto single scapegoats conceals the fact that the entire field—including the seemingly uninvolved—resonates and sustains the system. Resonance is not tied to volume, but to the field of confirmation.

* **Invisible Complicity:**  
  Those who tolerate dissenting voices but do not actively support them become part of the exclusion dynamic. Responsibility is shared in the resonance field, even if it becomes visible in different ways. Guilt is individualized—the resonance field integrates. Systemic complicity manifests itself in omission, silence, and following along.

* **Systemic Blindness:**  
  Focusing on single perpetrators and “culprits” prevents recognition of one's own involvement. The resonance field makes clear: Every passive affirmation, every act of looking away, every adaptation to language rules strengthens the group dynamic of exclusion. Blindness to one's own resonance is itself part of the dynamic.

* **Transformation Through Self-Observation:**  
  Self-awareness becomes the systemic corrective: The central question is not “Who is guilty?”, but: Where, how, and why do I resonate? Which collective patterns do I perpetuate by not intervening or by letting dissenting voices go unheard? Transformation begins by recognizing one’s own share in the whole.

## Resonance Rule: The Systemic Resolution of the System Game

**Whoever excludes, reinforces. Whoever projects, binds. Whoever fights, confirms.**  
The resonance rule breaks the game of guilt and perpetrator projection by revealing a fundamental insight: **Group belonging is systemically invariant.** It encompasses **all parts**—even those that are repressed, denied, or labeled as “evil.” Whoever tries to exclude unwanted parts not only misunderstands their belonging but **reinforces them through exclusion**.

---

### 7. The End of the System Game

The conventional power game operates through fragmentation and splitting of the overall field:

* **Perpetrators are externalized:** Destructive potential is projected onto individuals or groups and excluded from one’s own identity. The system relieves itself by locating “evil” outside.
* **Guilt is shifted:** Responsibility for systemic dynamics is offloaded onto scapegoats, outsiders, or minorities. The field appears pure, while the shadow aspects continue to operate in the excluded.
* **One’s own shadows are projected:** Everything that cannot be integrated into one’s own system—fear, insecurity, aggression, doubt—appears as a characteristic of the other. Projection stabilizes group boundaries but prevents integration and development.

But in the resonance field: **Every exclusion generates a feedback loop.**
The *fought*, the repressed, remains systemically effective—it returns as a shadow, as a counterforce, as an unconscious dynamic in the field. Projection unconsciously binds the excluded to the system and blocks real transformation. The split creates stabilization of the repressed dynamic, not its resolution.

> **Whoever excludes, loses control—not power. For power remains in the resonance field; control breaks upon it.**

In the complete resonance field, every voice, every element, every position remains part of the whole—regardless of evaluation, visibility, or belonging. The dissolution of the old system game begins where integration of the excluded becomes possible and the field recognizes itself as an indivisible whole. Transformation does not happen through further exclusion, but through acknowledgment of the systemic invariance of all group elements.

---

### 8. Responsibility Instead of Guilt

The resonance rule transforms the understanding of responsibility and guilt within the collective field:

* Responsibility is decoupled from the question of moral guilt: It is not about confessing individual guilt, but about recognizing oneself systemically as part of an indivisible resonance field.
* Systemic **self-inclusion** means that even the unwanted, repressed, or rejected parts are acknowledged as belonging to the group—regardless of evaluation, sympathy, or moral judgment.
* Real change does not arise from guilt projection or moral condemnation, but from **structural transparency**: making visible one's own participation, mutual shaping, and the invisible dynamics within the whole field.
* Responsibility in the resonance field means accepting one’s own share of the whole—not as a burden, but as a possibility for joint transformation.

The resonance rule remains systemically inviolable: Every attempt to evade responsibility or exclude parts binds them paradoxically even more deeply into the field. Responsibility means carrying the whole as a whole—and thus opening up the space for integration, development, and new forms of community.

### 9. Transformation Through Recognition

True change begins where the “Other”—the rejected, excluded, misunderstood—is no longer excluded or fought. In the resonance field, transformation does not result from further fragmentation, but from the conscious recognition of all components of the system.

* **It belongs.**  
  Every position—explicit or implicit, popular or unpopular, loud or quiet—is part of the systemic overall structure. Exclusion is an illusion: the rejected remains effective and shapes the field in hidden ways.

* **It is already there.**  
  What is fought, denied, or projected is already part of collective reality. The resonance rule shows: No part disappears through repression. The invisible continues to work in the shadows.

* **It resonates within the field.**  
  Every voice, every perspective, every shadow part shapes the group’s resonance field—regardless of evaluation or visibility. Resonance is all-encompassing and includes even what appears foreign.

> “It is not exclusion that changes the field, but the recognition of its full structure.”

Transformation begins when the whole is seen as a whole—when integration overcomes splitting and all parts are acknowledged as elements of the one resonance field. Only through this recognition do development, healing, and mutual growth become possible.

### 10. Consequence: Paradigm Shift

Insight into the resonance field marks not merely an ethical advance, but a **systemic inescapability**:

* No power game can be sustained once the whole is visible and recognized as an indivisible structure. Fragmentation, exclusion, and projection lose their effect, because the resonance field integrates every movement, every voice, every shadow.
* No perpetrator image can persist when all parts—even those repressed, denied, or projected—are reflected in the resonance field and recognized as part of the system. The function of the scapegoat ends when the field understands itself as a whole.
* No manipulation remains effective in the long term once projection is understood and seen as feedback. Every exclusion, every deception, every shift of responsibility returns to the system through the resonance law—making repression impossible.

---

**The resonance rule not only ends the game—it makes it obsolete.**  
For it reveals: *Whatever is excluded returns more forcefully—until it is recognized and integrated.*

This is the systemic lever that changes the world:  
The paradigm shift from the game of power and guilt to the integration of the whole within the resonance field.

### 11. Conclusion: Recognizing Instead of Fighting

The repeated fight against evil—whether in the individual, the group, or the societal field—leads to the reproduction of the structure when resonance and integration are lacking. The resonance field offers a systemic alternative: Whoever recognizes no longer needs to fight, but can absorb, understand, and resolve dynamics.

* The future does not belong to the self-righteous, but to those who recognize themselves in the Other, endure tension, and transform it—without masks, projection, or defense mechanisms.
* The resonance rule remains systemically invariant: Group belonging includes every element—regardless of language, evaluation, or perspective. Exclusion generates feedback, integration enables development.

#### Glossary

* **Resonance Field:** A system in which all elements—explicit and implicit—are interwoven and exert mutual effects.
* **Projection:** Splitting off one’s own parts and attributing them to others; a mechanism of shifting blame.
* **Gaslighting:** Systematic distortion and devaluation of others' perceptions to stabilize one's own system.
* **Self-Inclusion:** Acknowledgment and integration of one’s own shadow, conscious belonging to the whole.
* **Language Regulation:** The prescription of certain ways of speaking as markers of belonging and loyalty.
