## Perpetrator Projection in the Guise of Antifascism

**Systemic Dynamics of Narcissistic Power in Moral Language**

---

### 1. Introduction: The Mask of Morality

Not everyone who speaks of evil is fighting it. Evil reveals itself less in appearance than in structure. Authoritarian systems learn to legitimize themselves not through open violence, but through moral or linguistic rhetoric. Those who stage themselves as fighters against darkness become deaf to their own shadow. Perpetrator projection becomes an invisibility cloak—the destructive potential appears only on the outside. The resonance rule applies: Even what is morally excluded remains systemically part of the field.

---

### 2. Structural Features of Narcissistic Systems

* **Projection and Reversal:** The unwanted is split off, fought against, and located externally. Projection serves self-cleansing—guilt and danger are always attributed to the "others." Systemically, what is split off remains effective within the resonance field.
* **Language Regulation and Pressure for Conformity:** Not truth, but formal compliance counts. Those who do not use the “correct” words or pronouns fall under suspicion—regardless of their content. Belonging is defined by language, not arguments. Language serves as a marker: Those who refuse the new language are marked as outsiders. In authoritarian systems, linguistic conformity is an early and revealing instrument of power. In Hitler’s time, the salute served as a test of belonging—today there are other linguistic signals.
* **Gaslighting:** The perceptions of others are systematically distorted. Critical thoughts and experiences are reinterpreted, victims declared perpetrators, doubt is seen as attack. The field loses shared reality.
* **Moral Self-Immunization:** Criticism is not answered argumentatively, but morally devalued. Those who contradict are deemed inhuman, dangerous, outside the permissible discourse.
* **Systemic Loss of Resonance:** The feedback between individual and group breaks down—learning and development are prevented. The group becomes static, shadows continue to act systemically.

---

### 3. Antifascism as Narcissistic Camouflage

Antifascism is historically necessary and remains systemically relevant. Yet authoritarian dynamics can co-opt its terms:

* The concept of fascism is expanded and loses analytical sharpness.
* Antifascist rhetoric can serve as a projection screen: Those with dissenting opinions are labeled “right-wing.” One’s own actions remain unquestioned.
* Plurality and differentiated discourse are replaced by binary friend-foe thinking. The group perceives itself as a bulwark, while actually losing resonance and tolerance for ambiguity.
* Linguistic markers are introduced as tests: Those who do not participate are excluded. Language itself creates the boundary—no longer arguments, but correct speech determines belonging.

Thus, a system can become totalitarian while presenting itself as a defender of openness and diversity. The resonance rule also applies here: What is excluded remains part of the field and continues to act as a shadow.

### 5. Paths to Systemic Resolution

* **Self-Inclusion instead of Enemy Image Cultivation:** The group recognizes itself as a resonance field that also integrates its own shadows and errors. Further development becomes possible when the foreign is seen as part of oneself.
* **Mirror Work:** Foreignness and irritation are opportunities for self-knowledge. The group asks: What in me resonates with what I reject?
* **Resonance Ethics:** Not pure attitude or correct confession counts, but the quality of the relationship and the ability to withstand and productively transform tension and difference.
* **Systemic Dialogue:** Dissent is seen as an opportunity to broaden perspective, not as a threat.
* **Linguistic Resonance:** The diversity of language is recognized as a resource—not as a threat. Linguistic difference generates insight, not exclusion.

The alternative to projection is not agreement, but presence: In the resonance field, difference is used as a resource. The resonance rule guarantees: Every element remains part of the whole, regardless of evaluation, language, or position.

---

### 6. Symbolic Self-Positioning as a Strategy of Deception

Totalitarian and manipulative systems conceal their exercise of power not only with morality, but also with symbolic identification and language:

* They style themselves as part of the “common people,” as the voice of the oppressed, as the protector of the people.
* **Hitler’s Self-Presentation:** Frontline soldier, friend of workers, lover of nature—in reality, leader of a destructive, ideologically driven apparatus of annihilation.
* **Modern Party and State Rhetoric:** Terms like social justice, solidarity, peace, or the duty to use certain forms of language are used as rhetorical masks. In practice, however: military build-up, exclusion of critical voices, management of scarcity and insecurity.
* **Linguistic Belonging as an Instrument of Power:** Those who do not adopt the prescribed language are excluded—language becomes a filter of belonging and a tool of control.

What appears as help becomes subtle administration and perpetuation of powerlessness:

* Low earners are pacified by redistribution, not empowered.
* War is labeled a protective measure, but creates new dependencies and destroys living environments.
* Democracy and diversity are invoked symbolically, but in practice are undermined by language regulations, secret negotiations, networks, and lack of transparency.

**Resonance is absent where symbolic identification and linguistic uniformity replace reality.**
At the end stands disappointment—the end of deception, an invitation to new clarity. The resonance rule applies: Systemic belonging remains, even when identification is illusory and language becomes exclusive.

---

### 7. Conclusion: Recognizing Instead of Fighting

Fighting evil often leads to its reproduction—if the structure remains unchanged. The resonance field offers an alternative: Those who recognize do not need to fight, but can integrate and dissolve dynamics.

* The future belongs not to those who consider themselves better, but to those who can recognize themselves in others and transform tension—without masks, without projection, without resistance.
* The resonance rule remains systemically invariant: Group belonging encompasses every element, regardless of language, perspective, or evaluation.

---

#### Glossary

* **Resonance Field:** A system in which all elements—explicit and implicit—interact with each other.
* **Projection:** Splitting off parts of oneself and attributing them to others.
* **Gaslighting:** Systematic distortion and devaluation of others’ perceptions.
* **Self-Inclusion:** Acknowledging one’s own shadow and belonging to the whole.
* **Language Regulation:** Prescribing certain ways of speaking as markers of belonging.

---

#### Case Studies

* **Historical:** The “German Salute” as a linguistic marker of belonging in National Socialism. Deviation meant sanction and exclusion.
* **Current:** The use of certain pronouns or forms of language as an entry ticket in progressive milieus. Those who refuse are marked as a threat to the collective.
* **Media:** Cancel culture and shitstorms against deviant language or opinion, regardless of the actual facts.
