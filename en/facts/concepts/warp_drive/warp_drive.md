# 🌀 Warp Drive

## Introduction

The **warp drive** is based on concepts from modern field theory and utilizes the targeted manipulation of spacetime structures through **controlled resonance fields** (cf. Alcubierre, 1994). Unlike conventional propulsion systems, the object does not travel classically through space but remains relatively stationary within an artificially generated **resonance bubble**. Spacetime is **contracted in front of** and **expanded behind** the object—the object "rides" on a spacetime wave (cf. Lämmerzahl & Hehl, 2004).

## 📐 Mathematical Model

The spacetime distortion follows a modified wave equation integrating field amplitude, damping, and variable warp frequency:

$$
\Psi(x, t) = A \cdot \sin\left(2\pi \cdot \mathbf{f}_\text{warp}(x, t)\right) \cdot e^{-σ \cdot x}
$$

**Variables:**

- **A** = field amplitude
- **𝒇₍warp₎(x, t)** = positional warp frequency
- **σ** = damping coefficient
- **ε** = resonance coupling constant
